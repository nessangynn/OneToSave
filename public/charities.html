UNICEF

Currently, UNICEF is on the front lines, fighting the novel coronavirus with supplies, support and lifesaving information. Unicef's vision is a better world for children everywhere.

UNICEF USA receives the highest ratings for transparency, accountability and administration. Of every dollar spent, 89 cents goes toward helping children. 8.1 cents goes to fundraising costs, and 2.9 cents on administration.

DIRECT RELIEF

Direct Relief works in the U.S. and internationally to equip doctors and nurses with life-saving medical resources to care for the world's most vulnerable people.

The organization is making sure the courage of health workers on the front lines is honored with meaningful support, and the people most at risk in this pandemic are cared for – regardless of politics, religion, or ability to pay.

RED CROSS

The American Red Cross prevents and alleviates human suffering in the face of emergencies by mobilizing the power of volunteers and the generosity of donors.​

Red Cross volunteers and staff work to deliver vital services – from providing relief and support to those in crisis, to helping you be prepared to respond in emergencies. The American Red Cross is part of the world's largest volunteer network found in nearly 200 countries.

WWF - WorldWildLife

The WWF's mission is to conserve nature and reduce the most pressing threats to the diversity of life on Earth.

WWF’s work has evolved from saving species and landscapes to addressing the larger global threats and forces that impact them.

WWF works to educate and influence people into making sustainable choices and decisions, including those who work in business and make decisions around the use of natural resources, and those who work in government and set policy that impacts nature.

MARY's PLACE

Mary’s Place provides safe, inclusive shelter and services that support women, children and families on their journey out of homelessness.

Mary’s Place has helped hundreds of women and families move out of homelessness into more stable situations.

The women’s drop-in Day Center in downtown Seattle provides meals, showers, laundry, access to resources, safety and relationship to over a hundred women each day.
